Yadier Álvarez[edit]

This name uses Spanish naming customs: the first or paternal family name is Álvarez and the second or maternal family name is Ventosa.

Yadier Álvarez Ventosa (born March 7, 1996) is a Cuban baseball pitcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Álvarez defected from Cuba to pursue a career in Major League Baseball (MLB).[1][2] He has a fastball that touches 98 miles per hour. Scouts believe he has number two starter upside and one National League official has said that he is the best 18-year-old pitcher he had ever seen.[3] He received interest from numerous MLB teams including the Philadelphia Phillies, Los Angeles Dodgers and Arizona Diamondbacks.[4][5]

Ranked as the second best prospect by mlb.com heading into the 2015 international signing period, Álvarez signed with the Dodgers on July 2, 2015, for a $16 million bonus.[6][7] He made his professional baseball debut for the Dodgers Arizona rookie league affiliate on June 20, 2016 and struck out seven while only allowing one hit in 3 2⁄3 innings pitched.[8] In five starts for the team, he was 1–1 with a 1.80 ERA and 57 strikeouts.[9] He was promoted to the Great Lakes Loons of the Midwest League and struck out 10 in his debut for the Loons on July 21, 2016.[10] He made nine starts for the Loons with a 2.97 ERA and 55 strikeouts in only 39  1⁄3 innings.[11] In 2017, he was promoted to the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the California League and chosen to represent the world team at the All-Star Futures Game.[12]

Álvarez began 2017 with the Quakes and was promoted to the Double-A Tulsa Drillers at mid-season. Between the two leagues he made 18 starts (and three relief appearances) and was 4–6 with a 4.68 ERA.[13]

Matthew Thomas Beaty (born April 28, 1993) is an American professional baseball third baseman for the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Beaty attended Dresden High School in Dresden, Tennessee. The Kansas City Royals selected him in the 48th round of the 2011 MLB draft,[14] but he attended Belmont University. The Dodgers selected him in the 12th round of the 2015 MLB draft.[15][16] In 2017, Beaty played for the Tulsa Drillers of the Class AA Texas League, and was named the league's player of the year.[17] He also won the league batting title by hitting .326.[18]

Yusniel Efraín Díaz Hechavarria (born October 7, 1996) is a Cuban professional baseball outfielder in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Díaz played for the Industriales of the Cuban National Series and hit .348/.448/.440 over 65 games as a rookie.[19] He was the favorite to win the Cuban National Series Rookie of the Year Award, but defected before the award was given out.[20]

Díaz signed with the Los Angeles Dodgers in November 2015.[21] The Dodgers assigned him to the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the Advanced Class-A California League to begin his professional career.[22] In 82 games for the Quakes in 2016, he hit .267 with eight homers and 54 RBI.[23] In 2017, he played in 83 games for the Quakes and 31 for the Double-A Tulsa Drillers and hit .292 with 11 homers and 52 RBI.[23]

Pablo Millán Fernández Rojas is a Cuban baseball pitcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Fernández played for Holguín of the Cuban National Series as a relief pitcher.[24] He defected from Cuba in July 2014,[25] and agreed to sign with the Los Angeles Dodgers in March 2015 for a reported $8 million signing bonus.[24] A formal signing was held up as he awaited a visa to travel to the United States and take his physical, which was finally approved on May 11.[26] he officially signed with the Dodgers on May 19.[27] He pitched in 10 games in the Dodgers farm system in 2015, (with the Arizona League Dodgers, Great Lakes Loons and Rancho Cucamonga Quakes) and was 3–2 with a 3.92 ERA.[28]

Fernandez underwent Tommy John surgery on May 12, 2016, causing him to miss the entire 2016 season.[29] He returned in 2017 to pitch 12 innings across five games in the Arizona League, allowing 18 earned runs to score for a 13.50 ERA.[28]

Paul Hoenecke[edit]

Paul Hoenecke

First baseman / Third baseman / Catcher

Paul Andrew Hoenecke (/heneki/; born July 8, 1990) is an American professional baseball corner infielder and catcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Hoenecke played high school baseball at West Bend High School, where he won a state championship and was selected as player of the year two years in a row.[30][31] He was a star pitcher for West Bend as well as being one of their top run producers.[32] As a junior, he set a school record with a .515 batting average and also was 9–1 with a 2.18 earned run average as a pitcher.[33]

Hoenecke was drafted by the Detroit Tigers, at the age of 17, in the 42nd round of the 2008 Major League Baseball draft.[33] He was offered a $40,000 signing bonus but did not sign.[30] He attended the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee to play for the Milwaukee Panthers baseball team in the NCAA Division 1 Horizon League. With the Panthers, Hoenecke played infield and, in his senior year, played catcher. Hoenecke finished four years of play with a .301 batting average in 211 games.[34] He also played for the Green Bay Bullfrogs of the Northwoods League in 2010 and 2011.[35]

Hoenecke was drafted by the Los Angeles Dodgers in the 24th round of the 2012 Major League Baseball draft.[36] He made his professional debut on June 21, 2012 with the Arizona League Dodgers. In the game, Hoenecke went four for five with a home run and four RBIs while playing left field against the Arizona League Brewers.[37] He would win the Arizona League batting title with a .382 average and ranked second in the league in OPS (1.064), third in total bases (107) and seventh in RBI (38).[38] He was promoted to the Ogden Raptors of the Pioneer League on August 31[39] and had two hits in five at-bats over three games.[40]

Hoenecke was assigned to the Great Lakes Loons of the Midwest League to begin the 2013 season[41] In a May game, he suffered a lacerated spleen after a collision with Julio Urías and spent most of the rest of the season on the disabled list.[42] In 43 games for the Loons he hit .230[43] and he returned to the team for the following season.[44] After having previously been primarily a first baseman, Hoenecke played mostly third base in 2014 and he hit .247 with 15 homers and 61 RBI. He also committed 19 errors in 111 games.[43][45]

In 2015, Hoenecke played in a few major league games in spring training[46] but was assigned to the Advanced-Class A Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the California League to start the season.[47] He spent a week in Double-A with the Tulsa Drillers in April before returning to the Quakes.[48][46] He had a sixteen-game hitting streak for the Quakes in June before it was snapped[49] He had a .295 batting average for the Quakes in 2015, with nine homers and 63 RBI,[43] helping the team win the California League championship.[50] Hoenecke was a MiLB.com Organization All-Star after both the 2014 and 2015 seasons.[39]

Hoenecke returned to the Quakes at the start of the 2016 season and was now listed as a catcher.[51] After only a few games, he was promoted to Tulsa[52] where he was selected to the mid-season all-star team.[53] On the season, he was in 54 games combined between Rancho Cucamonga and Tulsa and hit .269 with nine homers and 30 RBI.[43] In 2017, he was named to the mid-season Texas League all-star game.[54] He played in 48 games, hitting .220 with seven homers and 44 RBI.[43]

Paul Hoenecke is from Kewaskum, WI and is the son of Andrew and Shelly Hoenecke. He is a descendant of 18th and 19th century German mathematician, Carl Friedrich Gauss.[30]

Drew Hamilton Jackson (born July 28, 1993) is an American professional baseball shortstop for the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Jackson attended Miramonte High School in Orinda, California. He then enrolled at Stanford University and played college baseball for the Stanford Cardinal.[55]

The Seattle Mariners selected Jackson in the fifth round of the 2015 Major League Baseball (MLB) draft.[56] He played for the Everett AquaSox of the Class A-Short Season Northwest League,[57][55] and was named the league's Most Valuable Player.[58] Jackson opened the 2016 season with the Bakersfield Blaze of the Class A-Advanced California League.[59]

On March 1, 2017, he was traded to the Los Angeles Dodgers (along with Aneurys Zabala) for Chase De Jong.[60] He was assigned to the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes,[61] where he played in 66 games with a .254 batting average before his promotion to the Double-A Tulsa Drillers, where he played in 29 games and hit .234.[62]

His older brother, Brett, has played in MLB.[55]

Gavin Lux[edit]

Gavin Lux

Gavin Thomas Lux is a professional baseball shortstop in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization. He played high school baseball for Indian Trail High School and Academy in Kenosha, Wisconsin and was selected in the first round of the 2016 Major League Baseball Draft by the Dodgers. His uncle is Augie Schmidt.

Lux was one of the top fifty high school prospects heading into the 2016 draft.[63] He won the 2016 Wisconsin baseball Gatorade Player of the Year awards and Holy Rosary Sports Night Male Athlete of the Year Award in high school.[64][65] He had committed to Arizona State University[66][67] but was drafted by the Los Angeles Dodgers in the first round, 20th pick, of the 2016 Major League Baseball Draft.[68]

On June 16, 2016, Lux signed with the Dodgers for a $2.31 million signing bonus.[69] The Dodgers assigned him to the Arizona League Dodgers to begin his professional career.[70] At the end of the season he was promoted to the Ogden Raptors of the Pioneer Baseball League.[71] Between the two levels, he played in 56 games with a .296 batting average and 21 RBI.[72]

Lux got a late start on the 2017 season as he dealt with a rib issue in spring training[73] but he was assigned to the Class-A Great Lakes Loons of the Midwest League on April 19.[74] He played in 101 games for the Loons, hitting .244 with seven homers and 39 RBI.[72]

Trevor Scott Oaks is a professional baseball pitcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization. He was drafted by the Dodgers in the 7th round of the 2014 MLB Draft out of California Baptist University. He had previously played in high school at Woodcrest Christian High School in Riverside, California.

Oaks began his professional career with the Ogden Raptors in 2014, where he was 5–2 with a 6.81 ERA in 14 games.[75] In 2015, he pitched in 23 games (21 starts) between the Great Lakes Loons and the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes and had a 8–5 record and 2.65 ERA.[75] For the 2016 season, he pitched in four games for the Quakes, and 10 each for the Tulsa Drillers and Oklahoma City Dodgers, with a 14–3 record and 2.74 ERA.[75] In 2017, he made 15 starts (and one relief appearance) for Oklahoma City and was 4–3 with a 3.64 ERA.[75] His season was cut short by an oblique injury suffered in July.[76]

Donald Scott Peters (born December 12, 1995) is an American professional baseball outfielder in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

Peters attended Glendora High School in Glendora, California. He was drafted by the Chicago Cubs in the 36th round of the 2014 Major League Baseball Draft, but did not sign and attended Western Nevada College, where he played college baseball. After one year at Western Nevada, he was drafted by the Texas Rangers in the 36th round of the 2015 MLB Draft, but again did not sign and returned to Western Nevada. In 2016, he was the Scenic West Athletic Conference Player of the Year.[77][78] After the season, Peters was drafted by the Los Angeles Dodgers in the fourth round of the 2016 MLB Draft and signed.[79]

Peters made his professional debut with the Ogden Raptors.[80] He started 2017 with the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes and was named to the California League mid-season all-star team.[81] He hit .276 with 27 homers and 82 RBI in 132 games[82] and at the end of the season, he was named to the post-season all-star team and was selected as the California League Most Valuable Player.[83]

Edwin Gabriel Ríos (born April 21, 1994) is an Puerto Rican baseball first baseman and third baseman in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization. Ríos was drafted by the Dodgers in the 6th round of the 2015 MLB Draft out of Florida International University.

Ríos began his professional career with the Arizona League Dodgers in 2015 but was quickly promoted to the Ogden Raptors of the Pioneer Baseball League. He hit .253 in 22 games for the two teams.[84] He began 2016 with the Great Lakes Loons of the Midwest League, hitting .252 with six homers and 13 RBI in 33 games,[84] earning a promotion to the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the California League, where he hit .367 with 16 homers and 46 RBI in 42 games.[84] He was promoted again, to the Tulsa Drillers of the Texas League. He was named the Dodgers organizational minor league player of the year for 2016.[85] Ríos was named as a starter in the Texas League mid-season all-star game in 2017.[54] For the season, he played in 128 games between Tulsa and AAA Oklahoma City and hit .309 with 24 homers and 91 RBI.[84]

Jordan Sheffield[edit]

Jordan Sheffield

Bats: Right Throws: Right

Jordan Ladon Sheffield (born June 1, 1995) is an American professional baseball pitcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization. He played college baseball at Vanderbilt.

Sheffield attended Tullahoma High School in his hometown of Tullahoma, Tennessee. Before graduating, it was discovered that he needed Tommy John surgery, which caused him to fall in the draft.[100] He was selected by the Boston Red Sox in the 13th round of the 2013 Major League Baseball draft, but did not sign and chose to attend Vanderbilt University. After sitting out the 2014 season recovering from surgery, Sheffield made 6 starts and 16 relief appearances for Vanderbilt in 2015. In 60 total innings, he would post a 5–2 win–loss record, 2.85 earned run average (ERA), and 55 strikeouts.[101] Sheffield made 16 starts in the 2016 season, and pitched to an 8–6 record, 3.01 ERA, and 113 strikeouts in 101 2⁄3 innings.[101]

Heading into the 2016 Major League Baseball draft, Sheffield was ranked as one of the top available players by MLB[102] and he was drafted by the Los Angeles Dodgers in the first round (36th pick).[103] He announced on June 25 that he had signed with the Dodgers.[104] Sheffield made one start for the rookie-class Arizona League Dodgers and seven for the Class-A Great Lakes Loons. He was 0–1 with a 3.75 ERA in 12 innings.[105]

Sheffield returned to Great Lakes to start the 2017 season,[106] where he made 20 starts and was 3–7 with a 5.04 ERA.[105] He was promoted to the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the California League at the end of the season, where he made four starts (and one relief appearance) and was 0–2 with a 8.00 ERA.[105]

His brother, Justus, is a pitcher in the New York Yankees organization.[107][108]

William Dills Smith (born March 28, 1995) is an American baseball catcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.[109] He was drafted by the Dodgers in the first round of the 2016 Major League Baseball Draft out of the University of Louisville.[110] Smith signed on July 17, 2016 for a $1.775 million signing bonus.[111] Smith began his professional career with the Ogden Raptors of the Pioneer Baseball League[112] and was then quickly promoted to the Class-A Great Lakes Loons of the Midwest League.[113] He played in seven games for Ogden, 23 for Great Lakes and 25 for the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes of the California League, hitting a combined .246.[114] He was named to the California League mid-season all-star team in 2017.[81] He hit .232 in 72 games for the Quakes with 11 homers and 43 RBI.[114] He was promoted to the Double-A Tulsa Drillers of the Texas League in July, but suffered a fractured hand after getting hit by a pitch in his debut with the Drillers and he spent the rest of the season on the disabled list.[115]

Mitchell White (born December 28, 1994) is an American professional baseball pitcher in the Los Angeles Dodgers organization.

White attended Bellarmine College Preparatory in San Jose, California and played college baseball at Santa Clara University. He missed his freshman season in 2014 after undergoing Tommy John Surgery. He returned in 2015 as a relief pitcher and in 2016 became a starter.[116] After his sophomore season, he was drafted by the Los Angeles Dodgers in the second round of the 2016 Major League Baseball Draft.[117]

White made his professional debut with the Arizona League Dodgers. After two starts he was promoted to the Great Lakes Loons and ended the season with the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes.[118][119] He did not allow an earned run in 22 innings over 11 games (six starts) for the three teams in 2016.[120] In 2017, he made 19 starts across three levels again, with nine for the Quakes, seven for the Tulsa Drillers and three rehab appearances for the Arizona Dodgers. He was 3–2 with a 2.93 ERA.[120]
